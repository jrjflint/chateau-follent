Privacy Policy

Chateau Follent respects your privacy and is committed to protecting your personal information. This policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website, join our waitlist, attend events, purchase products, or otherwise engage with us.

Last updated: March 30, 2026.

Our Privacy Principles

Chateau Follent aims to handle personal information in line with the Privacy Act 1988 (Cth) and the Australian Privacy Principles (APPs). We seek to collect only what we reasonably need, keep it secure, and give you clear choices about how we communicate with you.

What Information We Collect

Depending on how you interact with us, we may collect:

  • Identity details (such as your name).
  • Contact details (such as email address, phone number, and postcode).
  • Order and transaction details for purchases and bookings.
  • Age confirmation details where required by law for alcohol-related services.
  • Communication history with our team.
  • Website usage information, including IP address, browser type, and pages viewed.

How We Collect Information

We collect personal information when you:

  • Submit a form on our website (including the waitlist form).
  • Make an enquiry, booking, or purchase.
  • Subscribe to updates or marketing communications.
  • Interact with us in person, by phone, email, or social media.
  • Use our website, where cookies and analytics tools may operate.

How We Use Personal Information

We may use your information to:

  • Provide products, services, event experiences, and customer support.
  • Process payments, bookings, and fulfilment tasks.
  • Verify legal purchasing age where required.
  • Send updates about releases, events, and Chateau Follent news.
  • Improve our website experience, performance, and communications.
  • Detect and prevent fraud, misuse, or security incidents.
  • Comply with legal and regulatory obligations.

Direct Marketing and Communication Preferences

If you opt in, we may send you marketing messages about vintages, experiences, and related announcements from Chateau Follent. You can unsubscribe at any time by using the unsubscribe link in our emails or by contacting us directly.

Cookies and Analytics

We use cookies and similar technologies to help our website function, understand visitor behaviour, and improve usability. You can manage cookie settings through your browser. If cookies are disabled, some site features may not operate as expected.

Disclosure to Third Parties

We may share personal information with trusted service providers who assist with operations such as website hosting, analytics, payments, communications, logistics, or event administration. We do not sell your personal information.

In some cases, service providers may handle information outside Australia. Where this occurs, we take reasonable steps to ensure that privacy safeguards are maintained.

Data Security and Retention

We use administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal information from misuse, interference, loss, unauthorised access, modification, and disclosure.

We retain personal information only for as long as reasonably needed for business or legal purposes, and then securely delete or de-identify it.

Your Rights

You may request to:

  • Access personal information we hold about you.
  • Correct inaccurate or incomplete information.
  • Update your marketing preferences or unsubscribe.
  • Raise a privacy concern or complaint.

Children's Privacy

Chateau Follent services are intended for adults. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children where doing so would be inconsistent with applicable laws.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in law, technology, or our business practices. The latest version will always be published on this page with the updated date.

Contact Us

For privacy requests, corrections, or complaints, contact us at privacy@chateaufollent.com.au.

If you are not satisfied with our response, you may contact the Office of the Australian Information Commissioner (OAIC) via oaic.gov.au.

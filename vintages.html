2025 Vintage

The 2025 season focuses on scaling production volumes while keeping experimentation alive across each colour family. All quantities are expressed as finished litres alongside their approximate 750 mL bottle equivalents.

Whites

Moscato

Production volume: 3.75 L (5 bottles).

Bottled on 23 November 2025, this off-dry Moscato was crafted to drink now and enjoy over summer.

It blends Black Muscat and Muscat grapes from the Riverland sourced at the markets.

The tiny run follows an unfortunate accident that broke the glass carboy and cost 23 litres of wine.

Moscato Piquette

Production volume: 10 L (≈13 btls).

A low-intervention spritz crafted from the pressed Moscato skins to showcase summer aromatics in a feather-light frame.

QR and postcard anchors for this bottling are listed in marketing-links.md so new collateral stays in lockstep with the vintage archive.

Vermentino

Production volume: 21 L (28 bottles).

Picked in Mudgee on 6 February 2025, this Vermentino came in at 10 Baumé with a pH of 3.0 after crushing and basket pressing.

Kviek Hornindal yeast drove a warm garage ferment to suit limited temperature-control equipment, shaping a textured, alternative expression at a breezy 10% ABV for easy summer drinking.

Rosé

Rosé

Production volume: 25.5 L (34 bottles).

Sangiovese from Mudgee (picked 6 February 2025 at 9 Baume, pH 3.0) and Cabernet Sauvignon from Mudgee were fermented cool with Enogerm Syrah yeast. To lift the sugar, Cabernet Sauvignon picked 8 March 2025 at 14.6 Baume was blended in, using the saignée method to take juice at pH 3.55. The final blend leans darker in colour, with lees stirring to build texture into a dry rosé at 12% alcohol, offering contrast to the classic salmon-pink style.
